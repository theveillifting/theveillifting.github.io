About Me

✦

My name is Katherine Anne Brenner, but I’m called Kathy. This is strange to me, because the name Kathy looks, through my synaesthesia, very “yellow” and I prefer the more colourful Katherine (visually). But Kathy feels more comfortable because it’s easy to say. In any case, Katherine means “pure one”, which is a lot to live up to. But I yearn to express myself with a pure heart.

Who am I? If I zoom out (or deeply in) what I have experienced in my deepest being is that “I am you, you are me, we are one … there is only God/Source.” Still, in all that, there is room for a woman named Kathy.

So, who is Kathy? I was born in 1977 in York, Pennsylvania, USA and I am an only child. I was a dancer from age 3–18. I went to majorette and dance school, but I choreographed my own dancing at home sometimes with scarves like the dancer Isadora Duncan. For most of my childhood, I lived in my own little world, feeling eccentric with my cat Maggie, and eventually becoming a synesthetic-music-connoisseur (I used to work at a music store). The first band I discovered and loved (outside my mom’s disco music) was the Sugarcubes from Iceland. I then got into Icelandic music. This led me to find a pen-pal there in 1996 to help me learn the language and then we ended up falling in love and marrying. I moved to Iceland in 2007 and went to university for anthropology, but I don’t consider myself an anthropologist. I’m just curious about humans and life in general.

I’m now a 48-year-old mother of a four-year-old girl. She is a miracle and I wouldn’t be writing this book if it wasn’t for the experience I had of being overcome with raw, primordial power when I gave birth to her in 2021. It was as if I was born again. My daughter is a free spirit like me, but I spent many years “hiding my lamp under a bushel” and denying my authentic self-expression. Connecting the dots of various sufferings in youth, including mental and physical illness that had appeared to take a chunk of my life, I now feel so enriched. In my teens, thanks to mononucleosis (which developed into chronic fatigue syndrome), I became a “hermetic hermit” and learned to appreciate reading books. I think that I entered, and stayed long in, a self-imposed shell to percolate and contract so that I would later expand further into maturity and gain wisdom that might not have otherwise developed in this lifetime. A few non-dual experiences and a great deal of healing transformed my once incapacitating OCD into a superpower for creativity and organisation. I’ve broken out of my shell.

I unveil my true self: I am passionate, creative, imaginative, curious about everything in life, especially God/Source, reality, and consciousness. I’m playful, colourful, filled with hope and radically optimistic. I’m a miner, I find gems everywhere. I’m an alchemist/poet, a weaver of words and worlds. I proclaim that magic is real! In a synaesthetic sense, my true self is “purple and ecstatic”!

I surrender to joy and light whenever possible, but I also find light in the darkness and possibilities for transmutation. The book cannot be divorced from my life, spirituality, and my inspirations: music, poetry, art, my synaesthesia and even cats. I hope that this book radiates infectious joy, love and beauty. I speak the language of the birds.

“To name a thing is to call it forth from the dark. To write it is to make it stay.”
